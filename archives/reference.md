# Reference

## UNGRUND — a system for absolute electronic music after Tietchens

### One sentence
**UNGRUND** is a composition system that treats sound as substrate rather than vehicle, generates all material by synthesis from first principles (no samples), composes by block rather than arc, and exposes its own structure to the ear as a measurement, not as an expression.

The name is Jakob Böhme's *Ungrund* — "unground", the abyss prior to determination — taken up by Schelling and Heidegger as the name for whatever precedes the subject/object distinction. Here it names the position the system claims for music: not feeling first, not figure first, not narrative first — material first, the abyss before any of these.

---

## A. Philosophical core

### Premise

UNGRUND inherits the discipline Asmus Tietchens carved out of post-Schaefferian electronics and extends it into territories he refused to occupy. From him we take: material as fact, ear-audited formalism, the block-form, spatial dryness, abstract titling, the open numbered series, scepticism as compositional temperature, and the refusal of redemption arcs. Where we depart: Tietchens always retained recorded material (hydrophone, piano, room) as one terminus of the practice; UNGRUND closes that door entirely. **Synthesis only.** The substrate must be electrical and arithmetical. There is no recorded world to point at; there is only the calculation, the oscillator, and the ear that audits them.

This closure is not a purity stunt. It is the philosophical move. Recorded material — even when processed beyond recognition, as Tietchens did — carries a residual indexical claim ("this is the trace of an event in the world"). UNGRUND voids that claim. Every sound in an UNGRUND piece is the audible side of a calculation, nothing more. The piece's relation to the world is not that of a recording; it is that of a thermometer — a calibrated measurement of a structure that has no extramusical referent.

### What is rejected

- **Expression.** No sound is justified by the composer's intention to convey anything.
- **Narrative.** No piece tells a story, traces an arc, builds and resolves a tension.
- **Melody as meaning.** A pitch sequence is a parameter walk, not a tune.
- **The romantic composer as self.** The composer is a calibrator of processes. The "self" that bourgeois aesthetics installed at the centre of the work is evicted.
- **Sample-as-found-object.** The Schaefferian *objet sonore* is honoured as a historical step but no longer admitted; recorded material carries indexical residue UNGRUND refuses.
- **AI mimicry.** Generative systems trained on prior music are inadmissible. UNGRUND uses computation, not learning; algorithms, not models.
- **Cinematic synth music.** Pad-and-shimmer ambient, atmospheric "scoring", drone-as-mood.
- **Mood.** A piece is not "dark", "warm", "uneasy". A piece is a configured substrate; affective adjectives are critics' shorthand and do not belong inside the practice.

### What is at the centre

- **Material itself.** The sine, the band of noise, the gap. These are the data.
- **The substrate** — electrical, frequency-space, statistical. Material is what UGens produce.
- **Non-human time scales.** A *monade* may sustain for five minutes; a *firnis* may pulse every 47 seconds; a *Halbwertszeit* process may take 30 minutes to decay through a single half-life. Human metric time (the bar, the phrase, the four-minute pop song) is incidental.
- **Processes that compose themselves once tuned.** The composer designs the rule and the boundary; the piece runs.

### Where this lands the listener

Not catharsis. Not contemplation-as-comfort. Not "atmosphere". The listener stands in a perceptual relation to a non-anthropic structure: a configuration of substrate elements arranged so the ear can register their positions, distances, and transitions. The ear is the instrument of measurement; the soul is not the receiver. This is what Tietchens meant by *Absolute Music* — *"it contains no message except an esthetic one"* — and what we extend by removing even the residual indexicality of recorded sound.

### Accepted lineage

Asmus Tietchens (1980–present, Hamburg) · Ralf Wehowsky / RLW / P16.D4 (Frankfurt) · Achim Wollscheid (Selektion) · Bernhard Günter (Trente Oiseaux) · Helmut Lachenmann's *musique concrète instrumentale* stance · Xenakis' GENDYN (computation as compositional substance) · the lowercase / microsound generation (Steve Roden, Richard Chartier, Taylor Deupree's drier work) · Wandelweiser-reductionists (Frey, Beuger, Pisaro) for the rhythm-with-silence but not for silence-as-metaphysics · Mark Fell's pattern aesthetics · Florian Hecker's structural surfaces.

### Rejected lineage

Post-romantic ambient (Stars of the Lid, Tim Hecker's emotional surface) · cinematic synth music · drone-as-mood · "generative" as decoration · sampledelia · vapor- and hauntology- aesthetics that depend on recognisable found material · any practice that hides decisions behind statistical clouds the ear cannot audit.

### Binding axiom

> **No sound is justified by feeling; every sound is justified by its position in a calibrated process audible to the ear.**

Everything below descends from this sentence.

## B. The axioms

**I. Material precedes meaning.**
Every sound is a position in a substrate. The substrate is not a medium for content — it *is* the content. The composer does not "express" with sounds; the composer arranges sounds so the substrate becomes audible.

**II. Synthesis only.**
No recorded material. No samples, no field recordings, no recorded instruments, no neural-network-generated audio trained on recordings. The substrate is electrical (oscillators, noise generators) and arithmetical (filters, envelopes, scheduling). When a sound is needed, it is *calculated*. This axiom is non-negotiable; a piece that violates it is outside UNGRUND.

**III. Audited determinism.**
Generation is parametric and deterministic where possible. Where randomness is admitted, it is *bounded* (uniform white over a known range; never a black-box generative model). Every random call is auditable by sweeping it and listening. Stochastic clouds whose individual events the ear cannot resolve are a Schaefferian temptation we refuse.

**IV. Form is block, not arc.**
A piece begins because it begins. A block of material holds until the rule says it ends. Blocks abut, interrupt, or yield to one another. There is no development, no climax, no recapitulation. A piece ends when its process exhausts the substrate, not when an arc resolves.

**V. Silence is a register.**
Silence is one of the seven audible dimensions, addressable like pitch or bandwidth. A piece may schedule silence the way it schedules a *binde*. The eighth sound-class — *zäsur* — names silence as event; it is composed, not implicit.

**VI. The catalogue is open.**
A piece is one episode of an ongoing constraint. UNGRUND outputs series, not works. A piece's title is its catalogue position (e.g. `ungrund.0001`), not its image. The system as a whole is unbounded; any individual piece is one configuration.

**VII. No metaphor.**
A sound is not "like" thunder, not "like" breathing, not "like" anything. A sound is the audible side of a calculation. Programme notes that say "evokes" or "suggests" or "feels like" describe the listener's drift, not the piece. Critics may write such notes; the practice does not produce them.

**VIII. The ear measures; the soul does not receive.**
The listener's task is perceptual calibration: where is this sound positioned, how does it move, when does it stop. Introspection is permitted but is not the criterion. A piece is well-made if a competent ear can audit its structure, not if it makes the listener feel.

## C. Material vocabulary — the eight sound-classes

The synthesised palette. These eight classes exhaust the system's vocabulary. A piece selects from them; a piece may not introduce a ninth. Each card specifies what the class *is*, how it is generated, what it *can* do, and what it *forbids itself*.

### 1. `monade` — sustained sine
- **Definition (perceptual):** a single, perceptually stable pitched tone with no internal modulation. The ear hears a frequency, hears it continue, hears it stop. Nothing more.
- **Synthesis premise:** `SinOsc.ar(freq)`, optionally with a slow (`Lag.kr`-smoothed) amplitude envelope; a single voice or a small chord (≤4 voices) of mutually consonant just-intoned ratios. No FM, no AM, no glide.
- **Behavioural envelope:**
  - Duration: 5 s – 5 min.
  - Frequency range: 40 Hz – 4 kHz. (Outside this, it is *binde* or *firnis*, not *monade*.)
  - Amplitude: 0.05 – 0.6 (linear); 12 dB range maximum within one realisation.
  - Attack / release: ≥ 0.5 s each. Hard onsets are forbidden.
- **Role:** structural ground. The class against which other classes register.
- **Forbidden moves:** glissando · FM/AM that audibly displaces pitch · stereo width effects (must be a point source) · reverb beyond a single short slap (≤ 30 ms, ≤ −20 dB).

### 2. `riß` — broadband impulse
- **Definition:** a short crack, a tear in the texture. Rate-of-change is the entire identity.
- **Synthesis premise:** `WhiteNoise.ar` or `PinkNoise.ar` gated by a `Env.perc` with attack ≤ 1 ms, release ≤ 50 ms, optionally HPF'd to remove sub-100 Hz body. Or `Impulse.ar` through a resonant filter at very high Q for a "click + ring".
- **Behavioural envelope:**
  - Duration: 5 ms – 80 ms total.
  - Density: never two within 200 ms of each other (anti-rhythm).
  - Amplitude: peak 0.3 – 1.0 (the only class permitted to peak near unity).
- **Role:** interruption, threshold-crossing event, punctuation.
- **Forbidden moves:** any pitched ringing audible as a definite note · any reverb tail longer than 100 ms · any rhythmic repetition (a *riß* must read as event, not pulse).

### 3. `flockung` — granular cluster of sines
- **Definition:** a small, audible cloud of short sine grains in adjacent harmonic positions. The ear can count perhaps 3–8 grains; the cluster is not a wall.
- **Synthesis premise:** bank of `SinOsc.ar` at jittered partials of a fundamental (e.g. partials 5–11 with ±2% frequency jitter), each gated by a brief `Env.perc` (atk 5–20 ms, rel 80–250 ms), staggered onsets within a 200 ms window. Implementable as a `Synth` cloud triggered by `Demand` or as a `Pmono` with `Pseq` of detunes.
- **Behavioural envelope:**
  - Cluster duration: 200 ms – 2 s.
  - Grain count: 3 – 8.
  - Fundamental frequency: 80 Hz – 1.2 kHz.
  - Amplitude: 0.05 – 0.25 per grain.
- **Role:** foreground event with internal texture; the "scattered" class.
- **Forbidden moves:** more than 12 grains (becomes a cloud, not a flocculation) · reverb (must be dry) · sustained drone (a *flockung* must end within 2 s).

### 4. `binde` — narrow-band noise
- **Definition:** a sustained, narrow band of noise centred at a fixed frequency. Sits like a coloured shadow of a pitch — recognisably tuned, but not a tone.
- **Synthesis premise:** `WhiteNoise.ar` or `PinkNoise.ar` through `BPF.ar` with very narrow Q (rq ≤ 0.05), centre frequency steady. Or `Resonz.ar` for sharper edge.
- **Behavioural envelope:**
  - Duration: 5 s – 60 s.
  - Centre frequency: 100 Hz – 6 kHz.
  - Q (rq): 0.005 – 0.05.
  - Amplitude: 0.05 – 0.4 (level after the filter — pre-amp gain typically must compensate).
  - Slow amplitude breathing allowed (period ≥ 4 s, depth ≤ 6 dB).
- **Role:** register — defines and holds a frequency zone.
- **Forbidden moves:** bandwidth modulation that audibly widens the band beyond 1/3 octave · centre-frequency glide · any modulation faster than 0.25 Hz.

### 5. `schliere` — drifting comb-filtered noise
- **Definition:** a slowly sweeping spectral streak; the listener hears a "comb" of present partials moving through frequency-space.
- **Synthesis premise:** `WhiteNoise.ar` through `CombC.ar` or a chain of `AllpassN.ar` with delay-time modulated by very slow LFO (period 20 – 240 s). The motion must be aperiodic to the ear (use LFN of incommensurate frequencies summed; never a clean sine LFO that the ear catches as periodic).
- **Behavioural envelope:**
  - Duration: 20 s – 10 min.
  - Comb fundamental sweep range: 200 Hz – 2 kHz.
  - LFO period: 20 s – 240 s.
  - Amplitude: 0.05 – 0.3.
- **Role:** continuum — slow motion that organises long stretches.
- **Forbidden moves:** any motion the ear can read as periodic · sharp onset (must fade in ≥ 4 s) · co-occurring with another *schliere* (one moving streak at a time).

### 6. `kratzer` — dry granulated noise burst
- **Definition:** a short, granular, unvoiced rasp. The acoustic feel of "scratching"; no pitch, no body, only surface.
- **Synthesis premise:** `WhiteNoise.ar` through `HPF.ar` (≥ 3 kHz), through fast amplitude gating (`LFNoise0.kr` at 20–80 Hz, exponentiated), then enveloped by `Env.perc` (atk 5–20 ms, rel 60–500 ms). Or many tiny gated noise grains in fast succession.
- **Behavioural envelope:**
  - Duration: 100 ms – 800 ms.
  - HPF cutoff: 3 kHz – 12 kHz.
  - Gate rate: 20 – 80 Hz.
  - Amplitude: 0.05 – 0.3.
- **Role:** texture event in the high band; the high-frequency counterpart to *riß*.
- **Forbidden moves:** any pitched component · any reverb · ever lasting longer than 1 s (becomes a *binde* if sustained).

### 7. `zäsur` — composed silence
- **Definition:** an interval of silence inserted into the score as an event with explicit duration. The other classes know about it; their schedules respect it.
- **Synthesis premise:** no UGens. Scheduled as a `Rest` in `Pbind` or as an explicit duration gap between blocks. Implementation: the master scheduler advances the clock by `dur` without spawning any voice.
- **Behavioural envelope:**
  - Duration: 1 s – 60 s.
  - Density per piece: at most one *zäsur* every 60 s of music.
- **Role:** structural event; the system's eighth letter. Marks a section boundary, a refusal, a held question.
- **Forbidden moves:** implicit silence — every silence longer than 1 s must be marked as a *zäsur* in the score · *zäsur* during a sustained *monade* or *binde* (you cannot cut into a held register; you can only end it and then *zäsur*).

### 8. `firnis` — ultrasonic shimmer
- **Definition:** a very high, very quiet sine cluster at the edge of audibility. Functions as a glaze on the top of the spectrum.
- **Synthesis premise:** small bank of `SinOsc.ar` (3–6 voices) at frequencies 12 kHz – 19 kHz, individually amplitude-modulated by very slow `LFNoise2.kr` (rate 0.05 – 0.4 Hz), each at amplitude 0.005 – 0.03 (pre-master). Must include a band-limiter / SineShaper for safety on playback systems.
- **Behavioural envelope:**
  - Duration: 30 s – ∞ (typically piece-long or none).
  - Frequency range: 12 kHz – 19 kHz strict.
  - Total amplitude: ≤ 0.08 at master.
- **Role:** the ceiling; a high-band that "varnishes" the piece.
- **Forbidden moves:** descending below 12 kHz (then it is *binde* or *monade*) · ever appearing alone (must accompany at least one other class) · pulsing on a regular period (motion must be drift).

### Summary table

| Class      | Domain | Duration | Freq band | Role |
|------------|--------|----------|-----------|------|
| `monade`   | pitch  | 5 s – 5 min | 40 Hz – 4 kHz | ground register |
| `riß`      | event  | 5 ms – 80 ms | broadband | interruption |
| `flockung` | event  | 200 ms – 2 s | 80 Hz – 1.2 kHz (+ harmonics) | scattered foreground |
| `binde`    | register | 5 s – 60 s | 100 Hz – 6 kHz | held zone |
| `schliere` | continuum | 20 s – 10 min | 200 Hz – 2 kHz (sweep) | slow motion |
| `kratzer`  | event  | 100 ms – 800 ms | 3 kHz – 12 kHz | high-band texture |
| `zäsur`    | silence | 1 s – 60 s | — | structural pause |
| `firnis`   | ceiling | 30 s – ∞ | 12 kHz – 19 kHz | upper glaze |

## D. Process catalogue — the six generative principles

A piece selects exactly one process. Combinations dilute. Each process has a rule, required inputs, a stopping condition, and an audible signature the listener can verify.

### Process 1. `substratumwalk`
- **Rule:** One sound-class is designated *Grund* (ground; a `monade` or `binde`). Other classes appear as triggered events when the system's running amplitude crosses class-specific thresholds (each class has a different threshold, set in the score). When a non-ground class triggers, it runs through its full duration once, then sleeps until the threshold is crossed again.
- **Required inputs:** 1 ground class (sustained) + 1–4 trigger classes (event or register).
- **Stopping condition:** the *Grund* envelope crosses below a noise floor (e.g. −60 dB) and stays below it for 8 s.
- **Audible signature:** the listener hears the ground; events erupt at moments that feel structural, not metric.

### Process 2. `halbwertszeit`
- **Rule:** Every active voice's amplitude (and a chosen secondary parameter — e.g. filter cutoff) decays toward a fixed centre value with an exponential half-life *t½*. New voices spawn only when the total system energy crosses *upward* through a defined threshold (i.e. the previous voice quieted enough that a fresh event "fits"). Spawned voices choose their class by walking through a fixed exclusion-set order.
- **Required inputs:** 2–5 sound-classes drawn from the event and register families; *t½* per parameter.
- **Stopping condition:** *K* consecutive seconds pass without spawning (default K = *t½* × 4).
- **Audible signature:** density decreases over time; the piece "thins out" by a measurable law.

### Process 3. `ausschluss`
- **Rule:** A subset *F ⊂ {1..8}* of forbidden classes is fixed. The piece walks through all 2^(8−|F|) inclusion subsets of the remaining classes, in Gray-code order (each step changes exactly one class on or off). Each subset is held for a fixed duration *d* before transitioning.
- **Required inputs:** the exclusion set *F*; the per-subset duration *d*; the order (Gray code is default).
- **Stopping condition:** all subsets exhausted.
- **Audible signature:** the texture changes by *one element* at a time; the listener perceives a combinatorial walk.

### Process 4. `kontaktmenge`
- **Rule:** Two or three sound-classes are each given a slowly drifting parameter (e.g. *binde*'s centre frequency, *monade*'s pitch). Each class is audible only when its parameter lies within a defined *contact zone* (a region of frequency-space). As drifts pass through the zone, voices fade in; as they leave, voices fade out.
- **Required inputs:** 2–3 register/continuum classes; the contact zone; the drift law per class.
- **Stopping condition:** all voices have departed the contact zone for *K* seconds (default K = 30).
- **Audible signature:** the piece breathes — overlapping presences appear and disappear by proximity in frequency-space.

### Process 5. `streifenraster`
- **Rule:** The piece is divided into a fixed time-grid of *N × M* cells (e.g. 8 columns × 4 rows = 32 cells). Each cell is "lit" by exactly one sound-class, scheduled by a pre-determined assignment (the score). Cells abut without crossfade; class changes between cells are clean cuts.
- **Required inputs:** the grid dimensions; the assignment table; the cell duration.
- **Stopping condition:** end of grid.
- **Audible signature:** the piece reads as a tiling; the listener perceives the discrete passage from one cell to the next.

### Process 6. `schichtung`
- **Rule:** *L* layers run simultaneously, each governed by its own clock with an incommensurate period (e.g. 17 s, 23 s, 29 s, 41 s — pairwise coprime). A layer's voice is *audible* only when its layer-amplitude (a slow LFN per layer) is above a layer-specific threshold. The layers never synchronise; the listener hears varying simultaneities.
- **Required inputs:** *L* (2–5); per-layer clock period; per-layer class; per-layer threshold.
- **Stopping condition:** total energy falls below a fixed threshold for *K* seconds.
- **Audible signature:** the piece feels stratified — layers come and go without alignment.

### Process selection guide

| If you want… | Use |
|--------------|-----|
| ground + event interruptions | `substratumwalk` |
| controlled thinning over time | `halbwertszeit` |
| combinatorial walk through a vocabulary | `ausschluss` |
| breathing overlaps in frequency-space | `kontaktmenge` |
| hard-edged tiling | `streifenraster` |
| polyrhythmic stratification | `schichtung` |

## E. Form catalogue — the five containers

A piece selects exactly one form. The form determines the temporal architecture; the process determines what fills it.

### Form 1. `Schicht` (single layer)
- **Duration:** 4 – 10 min.
- **Structure:** continuous; one process, 1–3 sound-classes.
- **Allowed processes:** any.
- **Role of silence:** *zäsur* permitted at most twice.
- **Stopping rule:** by the process's own stopping condition.
- **Experienced shape:** a single texture explored, exhausted, ended.

### Form 2. `Tafel` (table)
- **Duration:** 8 – 16 min total (8 études × 1 – 2 min each).
- **Structure:** sectional, 8 ordered études. Each étude features exactly one sound-class as soloist. The class order is fixed in the score.
- **Allowed processes:** each étude uses a miniaturised `substratumwalk` or `halbwertszeit` adapted to one class.
- **Role of silence:** a *zäsur* (≥ 4 s) between every étude.
- **Stopping rule:** end of 8th étude.
- **Experienced shape:** a Wittgensteinian board — eight discrete demonstrations, no commentary.

### Form 3. `Kontakt` (contact)
- **Duration:** 12 – 30 min.
- **Structure:** continuous.
- **Allowed processes:** `kontaktmenge` only.
- **Allowed classes:** 2 – 3, drawn from `monade`, `binde`, `schliere`, `firnis`.
- **Role of silence:** none scheduled; emergent silence may occur when no voice is in contact.
- **Stopping rule:** by `kontaktmenge`'s stopping condition.
- **Experienced shape:** a slow weather of presence.

### Form 4. `Raster` (grid)
- **Duration:** 20 – 45 min (32 cells × 40 – 80 s).
- **Structure:** strictly tiled; `streifenraster` process drives the grid.
- **Allowed processes:** `streifenraster` only.
- **Allowed classes:** all 8.
- **Role of silence:** any cell may be assigned `zäsur`; typically 4 – 8 of 32 are.
- **Stopping rule:** end of grid.
- **Experienced shape:** a survey of the vocabulary in 32 cells.

### Form 5. `Apophasis` (negative)
- **Duration:** 20 – 60 min.
- **Structure:** the listener is shown the full vocabulary in a one-minute prologue (a quick presentation of each of the 8 classes, separated by short *zäsuren*). The body of the piece then consists of *extended* sections, each featuring only one or two classes — i.e. the piece is defined by *which classes are absent*.
- **Allowed processes:** `ausschluss`, `schichtung`.
- **Allowed classes:** all 8 in the prologue; restricted subsets in the body.
- **Role of silence:** central; *zäsuren* mark every absence.
- **Stopping rule:** when the exclusion walk completes its determined path.
- **Experienced shape:** the listener perceives what is missing as much as what is sounding — Tietchens' "room between foreground and background" turned inside out.

### Form summary

| Form | Duration | Process(es) | Classes | Silence |
|------|----------|-------------|---------|---------|
| `Schicht` | 4 – 10 min | any | 1 – 3 | sparse |
| `Tafel` | 8 – 16 min | adapted per étude | 8 (one per étude) | between études |
| `Kontakt` | 12 – 30 min | `kontaktmenge` | 2 – 3 | emergent |
| `Raster` | 20 – 45 min | `streifenraster` | all 8 | 4–8 cells |
| `Apophasis` | 20 – 60 min | `ausschluss` / `schichtung` | full vocabulary referenced | central |

## F. Mapping methodology

### The seven audible dimensions

UNGRUND recognises seven perceptual dimensions. Every process parameter must map to one of them. Parameters that cannot be mapped are discarded.

| # | Dimension | Range | Notes |
|---|-----------|-------|-------|
| 1 | **Pitch / centroid** | 40 Hz – 19 kHz | for sines: actual freq; for noise: spectral centroid |
| 2 | **Bandwidth** | 1 Hz (pure tone) – broadband | controlled by filter Q / number of partials |
| 3 | **Event density** | 0 – 10 events/s | for event classes; 0 for sustained classes |
| 4 | **Attack-sharpness** | 1 ms – 10 s | envelope rise time |
| 5 | **Decay law** | linear / exponential / step | exponentially-weighted decay rate; or hard step |
| 6 | **Spatial position** | −1.0 (L) … +1.0 (R) | strictly limited; no surround |
| 7 | **Loudness register** | −60 dBFS – −6 dBFS | every class has its own permitted band within this |

### Mapping rule

> Every parameter exposed by a process must control exactly one named dimension. A parameter that would influence two dimensions simultaneously (e.g. amplitude that also widens bandwidth via filter saturation) is to be split or removed.

### Forbidden mappings

- **Pitch ↔ density:** never link a class's pitch to its event rate. The two dimensions must remain orthogonal in the listener's perception.
- **Decay law ↔ spatial position:** decays must not steer panning; spatial motion in UNGRUND is reserved for slow, intentional drift, not a side-effect.
- **Loudness ↔ attack-sharpness:** louder must not also mean sharper. The system avoids the gestural rhetoric this coupling produces.
- **Stochastic mapping of pitch:** a class's centre pitch is fixed in the score, not generated; only continuum classes (*schliere*) may drift, and only by their own slow LFN.

### Ranges per class

| Class | Centroid | Bandwidth | Density | Attack | Decay | Pan | Loudness band |
|-------|----------|-----------|---------|--------|-------|-----|---------------|
| `monade`   | fixed 40 Hz – 4 kHz   | ~0 (sine) | 0 (sustained) | ≥ 0.5 s | exp | ≤ ±0.2 | −24…−6 dBFS |
| `riß`      | broadband             | broad     | ≤ 5/s, with refractory | ≤ 1 ms | exp | ≤ ±0.6 | −12…0 dBFS peak |
| `flockung` | partial cluster       | discrete  | one cluster, 3–8 grains | 5–20 ms | exp | per-grain | −24…−9 dBFS |
| `binde`    | fixed 100 Hz – 6 kHz | very narrow (rq ≤ 0.05) | 0 (sustained) | ≥ 0.5 s | exp | ≤ ±0.3 | −24…−9 dBFS |
| `schliere` | drifting 200 Hz – 2 kHz | comb teeth | 0 (sustained) | ≥ 4 s | linear | 0 (centre) | −24…−12 dBFS |
| `kratzer`  | 3 kHz – 12 kHz        | broad-band HP | ≤ 4/s, with refractory | 5–20 ms | exp | ≤ ±0.4 | −24…−9 dBFS |
| `zäsur`    | —                     | —         | event-as-gap | — | — | — | −∞ |
| `firnis`   | 12 kHz – 19 kHz       | sine cluster | 0 (sustained), slow LFN AM | ≥ 8 s | linear | per-voice | −36…−18 dBFS |

## G. Notation — the UNGRUND score

The score is a structured text. The intended consumer is the SuperCollider system, but a competent reader can also perform it on other DSP systems. The dialect is JSON-like; a `.ungrund` file is parsed at piece load.

### Minimal grammar (informal)

```
piece <id> {
    form: <form-name>
    duration: <seconds>
    process: <process-name>
    classes: [<class-name>, ...]
    parameters: {
        <class-name>.<param>: <value> [, ...]
    }
    schedule: {                   // optional, for streifenraster and tafel
        <time-range>: <class-name> [<param-overrides>] [, ...]
    }
    seed: <integer>               // optional, for reproducibility of bounded randomness
}
```

### What the score fixes vs what the system decides

- **Fixed by the score:** form, process, class set, per-class central parameters, duration, stopping condition (implicit in the process), seed.
- **Decided by the system at run-time:** the exact onset of each event within a class's permitted density, the realised value of any bounded-random parameter (within the score's declared range), the exact phase of slow LFNs.

This split — score fixes the *what*, system fixes the *exact-when* within bounded windows — is the live edge of audited determinism. A piece is reproducible up to perceptually-irrelevant fluctuations.

### Example score (the primary implementation target — see Etude 1 below)

```ungrund
piece ungrund.0001 {
    form: schicht
    duration: 360
    process: substratumwalk
    classes: [monade, riß, firnis]
    parameters: {
        monade.freq:      94
        monade.amp:       0.4
        monade.atk:       6.0
        monade.rel:       12.0
        riß.threshold:    0.30   // trigger when running RMS crosses this
        riß.refractory:   8.0    // seconds minimum between events
        riß.amp_min:      0.4
        riß.amp_max:      0.85
        firnis.freqs:     [12640, 13110, 14790, 16030, 17880]
        firnis.amp_each:  0.015
        firnis.lfn_rate:  0.12
    }
    seed: 0x4E5A
}
```

The intent: a six-minute piece with a sustained 94 Hz *monade* as ground (deliberately picking up the project's existing `prototype.scd` 94 Hz frequency as a continuity gesture), occasional broadband *riß* events at audible amplitude thresholds, and a continuous *firnis* glaze across the top. Three classes, one process, one form. The whole machinery of UNGRUND demonstrated at minimum complexity.

## H. Three realised compositions — the etudes

Three concrete reference pieces. Each is fully specified. Together they exercise all 8 sound-classes, 3 of the 6 processes, and 3 of the 5 forms. The first is the **primary implementation target** for the SuperCollider analyst and developer; the second and third reuse its SynthDef bank with different patterns and form-level schedulers.

### ungrund.0001 — *schicht / substratumwalk / 94 Hz*

#### Primary implementation target

#### One sentence
A six-minute piece in which a sustained 94 Hz sine is the ground, broadband impulses interrupt when the local RMS crosses a threshold, and a quiet five-voice ultrasonic shimmer remains continuous throughout.

#### Constraint
- **Does not vary:** the *monade*'s pitch (94 Hz), the *firnis* voices' fixed pitches, the process (`substratumwalk`).
- **Does vary:** the timing of each *riß*, the realised amplitudes (within declared ranges), the *firnis* amplitude breathing.

#### Material
- **Pitch world:** `monade` at 94 Hz fixed; `firnis` at five fixed ultrasonic pitches (see score); `riß` is unpitched broadband. No melody. No harmony.
- **Time world:** absolute clock; 360 s total. Onsets within `riß` are pseudo-random within bounded density (1 event per 8–60 s).
- **Timbre / instrument(s):** pure sine (`monade`, `firnis`); HPF'd white noise impulse (`riß`).
- **Dynamic range:** master peaks at ≈ −6 dBFS; ground bed sits ≈ −15 dBFS; *firnis* sits ≈ −28 dBFS; *riß* peaks at ≈ −6 dBFS (the only sharp event class).

#### Process
`substratumwalk` with the *monade* as *Grund*. The system continuously measures the *monade*'s contribution to the master bus (post-envelope RMS over a 1.5 s window). When this RMS crosses 0.30 *upward*, a `riß` voice is spawned, provided no `riß` has been spawned within `refractory` (8 s). The `firnis` plays continuously from t = 4 s onward (after a 4-second fade-in), with its individual voice amplitudes modulated by independent LFN at rate 0.12 Hz. The *monade*'s 6 s attack and 12 s release frame the piece.

#### Form
Single block, single process. No subsections. No transitions. The piece begins with the *monade*'s slow fade-in, the *firnis* joins after 4 s, *riß* events begin to fire as the *monade* approaches steady-state, and the piece ends as the *monade* fades out at t ≈ 348 – 360 s.

#### Mapping

| Process parameter | Audible dimension | Range |
|-------------------|-------------------|-------|
| `monade.freq` | pitch (centroid) | fixed 94 Hz |
| `monade.amp` | loudness register | 0 → 0.4 → 0 (slow) |
| `monade.atk`, `monade.rel` | attack-sharpness, decay law | 6 s / 12 s (exp) |
| `riß.threshold` | event density (RMS trigger) | 0.30 |
| `riß.refractory` | event density (min interval) | 8 s |
| `riß.amp_min/max` | loudness register (per event) | 0.4 – 0.85 |
| `firnis.freqs` | pitch (5 fixed centroids) | 12.6 kHz – 17.9 kHz |
| `firnis.amp_each` | loudness register (per voice) | 0.015 |
| `firnis.lfn_rate` | decay law / slow drift | 0.12 Hz |

Every audible dimension declared by the system appears in this table.

#### Notation
Inline UNGRUND score (see Section G example). Saved alongside `prototype.scd` as `ungrund.0001.ungrund` (parsed by the SC implementation if a parser is built; otherwise the parameter values are duplicated into the `.scd` directly).

#### Performance / rendering notes
- Headphones or studio monitors with usable response above 12 kHz strongly preferred. On systems that roll off above 14 kHz, the *firnis* becomes inaudible — this is acceptable; the piece is not destroyed by its absence, only thinned.
- Tuning / calibration: master fader to leave 6 dB of headroom at peak.
- **Stopping condition:** at t = 360 s, the *monade*'s envelope reaches zero and all voices are freed. If a *riß* is currently in its release phase, it completes naturally and frees.

#### Listening references
- Asmus Tietchens, *Litia* (1983) — for the 94 Hz / Hamburg-axis continuity.
- Asmus Tietchens, *Zeta-Menge* (2006, Line) — for the "room between foreground and background" articulated by *monade* + *firnis*.
- Bernhard Günter, *Un peu de neige salie* (1993, Trente Oiseaux) — for *riß*-density and quiet master peaks.

- §Technique points 1, 3, 4 (sources kept clean; processing minimal; mid-low body with ultrasonic shimmer).
- §Why it matters items 1, 4, 5 (material discipline; spatial dryness; silence as structural pause — emergent here, not scheduled).

### ungrund.0002 — *tafel / eight études*

#### One sentence
A twelve-minute table of eight études, each ninety seconds long, each featuring exactly one of the eight sound-classes as soloist; the order of the études is fixed; *zäsuren* of six seconds separate them.

#### Constraint
- **Does not vary:** the order of the eight classes, the per-étude duration (90 s), the inter-étude *zäsur* (6 s).
- **Does vary:** the internal life of each étude (within its class's permitted parameters), the realised events for event classes.

#### Material
- **Pitch world:** each étude inherits its class's permitted ranges. The fixed values used per étude are listed in the schedule below.
- **Time world:** absolute clock; 8 × 90 + 7 × 6 = 762 s ≈ 12'42".
- **Dynamic range:** −36 dBFS – −6 dBFS overall; per-class as defined in Section F.

#### Process
A miniature `substratumwalk` or `halbwertszeit` adapted to one class per étude:
- For sustained classes (`monade`, `binde`, `schliere`, `firnis`), the étude is the class held for 90 s with its own internal slow life (envelope breathing, LFN drift).
- For event classes (`riß`, `flockung`, `kratzer`), the étude is `halbwertszeit` applied to event density: a high density at the start, decaying to one event every 20 s by the end.
- For `zäsur`, the étude is 90 s of silence (yes — silence itself takes a 90 s turn as soloist; this is the system's most literal statement of axiom V).

#### Form
Tafel. Strict 8-étude sequence, fixed order:
1. `monade` (94 Hz)
2. `binde` (centre 1100 Hz, rq 0.02)
3. `schliere` (sweep 200 → 1800 Hz over 90 s)
4. `firnis` (5 voices, as in ungrund.0001)
5. `riß` (`halbwertszeit` on density, t½ = 30 s)
6. `flockung` (one cluster every 5 – 20 s, fundamental cycling through 220, 277, 349, 415 Hz)
7. `kratzer` (`halbwertszeit` on density, t½ = 25 s, HPF 5 kHz)
8. `zäsur` (90 s)

#### Mapping

| Étude | Class | Primary audible dimension explored |
|-------|-------|-------------------------------------|
| 1 | `monade` | pitch register (fixed); envelope |
| 2 | `binde` | bandwidth (very narrow) |
| 3 | `schliere` | centroid drift |
| 4 | `firnis` | upper ceiling register; very slow density via LFN AM |
| 5 | `riß` | event density (decaying) |
| 6 | `flockung` | bandwidth (partial cluster); event-as-internal-density |
| 7 | `kratzer` | upper-band texture density |
| 8 | `zäsur` | silence as register |

Every audible dimension is exercised somewhere in the Tafel.

#### Notation
UNGRUND score:

## I. Why this matters — the philosophical close

UNGRUND extends Tietchens' practice along three axes he did not pursue.

**One**, it closes the synthesis-only door entirely. Tietchens kept the hydrophone as a permanent source; he kept the piano on *Notturno*; he kept Arcane Device's feedback rig on *dbl.fdbk*. UNGRUND admits none of these. The result is not "more pure" — purity is a romantic concept — but more *honest about what is happening*. There is no recorded reality being abstracted; there is only the calculation. The piece declares its own conditions of possibility on its face.

**Two**, it makes the system explicit and shareable. Tietchens audited everything by ear and kept the audit private; the listener experiences his judgements as decisions whose reasoning is sealed. UNGRUND exposes the vocabulary (8 sound-classes), the verbs (6 processes), the containers (5 forms), and the mapping (7 audible dimensions) in advance. The score is readable. The score is composable by others. The system is not the property of its inventor.

**Three**, it builds in the open-numbered series at the level of the system, not just the catalogue. Tietchens' *Hydrophonie 1–32+* is a series of pieces that share a method; UNGRUND is a method that knows itself as a series-generator and instruments its catalogue accordingly. Every UNGRUND piece is `ungrund.NNNN`; the numbers do not stop.

Relative to 2025 experimental electronic practice, UNGRUND positions itself against three dominant tendencies: (a) the AI-generative wave (rejected: opaque models, training data, the absence of an auditable rule); (b) the modular-synth nostalgia (rejected: gear fetish, the implicit narrative of the patch-as-journey); (c) the post-club emotionalism that has crept into "experimental" releases through Bandcamp tags (rejected: mood is not material). What remains is small and stubborn: the calculation, the rule, the ear.

## J. Honesty register

### Invented for this system

- The system's name: **UNGRUND** (the philosophical term is Böhme's; its application as a music-system name is new here).
- The eight sound-class names: `monade`, `riß`, `flockung`, `binde`, `schliere`, `kratzer`, `zäsur`, `firnis`. The German words are real common nouns; their use as sound-class names is original to this document.
- The six process names: `substratumwalk`, `halbwertszeit`, `ausschluss`, `kontaktmenge`, `streifenraster`, `schichtung`.
- The five form names: `Schicht`, `Tafel`, `Kontakt`, `Raster`, `Apophasis`.
- The three etude titles: `ungrund.0001`, `ungrund.0002`, `ungrund.0003`.
- The UNGRUND score DSL.

### Borrowed (with attribution)

- The *Ungrund* concept: **Jakob Böhme**, *De Signatura Rerum* (1622); developed by **F. W. J. Schelling**, *Freiheitsschrift* (1809); **Heidegger**, *Schelling-Vorlesung* (1936).
- The *Materialdenken* posture: **Theodor W. Adorno**, *Philosophie der neuen Musik* (1949). Tietchens fits this frame; whether he cites Adorno himself is `uncertain — verify` (per `docs/research.md`).
- The *Absolute Music* term: **Asmus Tietchens** (verbatim, *Psychedelic Baby Magazine* 2021 interview; see `docs/research.md`).
- The "spatial structures without being spacy" idea: **Asmus Tietchens** (paraphrased, on *Zeta-Menge*; see `docs/research.md`).
- The "album as collected études" form: **Asmus Tietchens** (*Notturno*'s eight *Studien*; *Beta-Menge*'s *Teilmenge*; see `docs/research.md`).
- The numbered-series catalogue architecture: **Asmus Tietchens** (*Hydrophonie*, *Menge*), with antecedents in **Karlheinz Stockhausen**'s *Studie I/II* and **Schaeffer/Henry**'s *Études de bruits*.
- The block-form, the rejection of narrative: **Wandelweiser** (Antoine Beuger, Michael Pisaro, Jürg Frey) — adopted as procedural ground, rejected as metaphysics (we do not treat silence as substance).
- Audited determinism vs stochastic clouds: **Asmus Tietchens** (*"I rely on my ears (!) and not (!) on algorithms or mathematics"*, OndaRock; see `docs/research.md`) and **Iannis Xenakis** (GENDYN as procedural counter-example).
- The 94 Hz reference: the project's existing `prototype.scd`.

### Deliberately rejected

- **Recorded source material** in any form. Even Tietchens' hydrophone is excluded. Reason: indexical residue. The substrate must be electrical.
- **AI-generated audio.** Reason: opaque models violate Axiom III (audited determinism).
- **Reverb beyond minimal slap.** Reason: imagined-space rhetoric, Schaefferian habit (see Axiom IV and the research note on Tietchens' spatial dryness).
- **Surround / multi-channel beyond stereo.** Reason: spatialisation is dimension 6, intentionally limited; surround introduces an immersive theatre UNGRUND refuses.
- **Wandelweiser's silence-as-substance.** Reason: silence is a register (Axiom V), not a metaphysical category. We use silence; we do not worship it.
- **Schimpfluch-Gruppe transgression.** Reason: transgression is gestural; UNGRUND is formal.
- **Mood adjectives in titling.** Reason: Axiom VII (no metaphor). A piece is `ungrund.0001`, not "Lament for the Drowned".
- **Open-ended improvisation.** Reason: Axiom III. A piece is a configured process, not a session.
