# Research

## Asmus Tietchens

### Position

- **Period**: late 20th-century / contemporary German experimental electronic — active since 1965, public discography 1980→present. Spans the 1970–2000 "plurality" period and continues into the 2000–present period.
- **Geography & scene**: Hamburg (not Köln, not Berlin, not Düsseldorf). Tietchens is geographically and aesthetically *adjacent* to the great German electronic-music institutions (WDR Köln / Stockhausen / Eimert; the Berlin school of Tangerine Dream / Schulze; the Düsseldorf axis of Kraftwerk / Cluster / Conrad Schnitzler) but belongs to none of them. After ~1984 his real scene is a North-Sea / Rhine corridor of small specialist labels: **Selektion** (Frankfurt, Achim Wollscheid / Ralf Wehowsky), **Die Stadt** (Bremen, Jochen Schwarz), **Auf Abwegen** (Köln, Till Kniola), and the American micro-edition label **Line** (Richard Chartier, Washington DC).
- **Day job / institutional position**: chemist by training (matriculated 1960s); for decades held a parallel professional life outside music (often summarised as "industrial chemistry" — *uncertain — verify the exact employer; the frequently repeated "Mobil Oil Hamburg" attribution circulates online but is not directly sourced in any interview I could verify*). Since 1990 he has taught **sound design / acoustics at the Hochschule für bildende Künste (HFBK), Hamburg**. In his own words from the Roedelius interview: *"My gigs and my lectureship in sound design at the University of Fine Arts in Hamburg bring in all the money."* (Electronic Beats interview)
- **Studio**: Audiplex Studio (initially with Okko Bekker, Hamburg) and later his private windowless studio: *"Here I've worked, secluded from the world, for more than two decades now. There are no windows facing the street. I work in total isolation from the outside world."* (Roedelius/Tietchens, Electronic Beats)
- **Prizes**: among the named recipients listed for the **Karl-Sczuka-Preis** (SWR, Donaueschinger Musiktage) for radio art — *uncertain — verify the exact year and the awarded work*. Multiple sources name him in the Sczuka winners list; the specific piece title/year was not pinned down here.

### Periodisation (four phases)

#### Phase 1 — Sky Records / "early melodic" 1980–1983
Quasi-pop synth miniatures produced after Peter Baumann (Tangerine Dream) heard a tape and offered to produce *Nachtstücke*. The four-LP tetralogy is the agreed canon:

- *Nachtstücke* (1980, Sky Records / Egg) — produced by Peter Baumann. Already abstract, but with timbral palette borrowed from Berlin-school synth music.
- *Biotop* (1981, Sky Records) — sixteen short electronic pop tracks, "immediate" but with unusual harmonic / melodic angles.
- *Spät-Europa* (1982, Sky Records) — third LP of the tetralogy.
- *In die Nacht* (1982, Sky Records) — *uncertain — verify*: Scaruffi lists this as part of the Sky run between *Spät-Europa* and *Litia*; other discographies fold it into the same year.
- *Litia* (1983, Sky Records 087, mixed at Audiplex Hamburg) — closes the Sky tetralogy. Tracks include *Zeebrügge*, *Pollys Square Dance*, *Ritual der kranken Freude*, *Litia*, *Auf elf*.

This phase is the only one in which a casual listener can hear "tunes". Tietchens distances himself from it later; the four albums were eventually reissued by Die Stadt in the 2000s as a careful canonical restoration.

#### Phase 2 — break with melody, 1984–1989
The decisive cut. *Formen letzter Hausmusik* ("Forms of last domestic music") on Steven Stapleton's **United Dairies** is the public turning point — released by an industrial label, no more chords, no more songs, only constructed sound-objects.

- *Formen letzter Hausmusik* (1984, United Dairies) — "based on ideas and experiments developed over 20 years" (Scaruffi). Drier, harder, formal.
- *Musik unter Tage* (1983, Aeon) — *uncertain — verify year*; subsonic / environmental.
- *Seuchengebiete* ("plague zones", 1985, A-Mission) — earliest published use of **water as source material** (the seed of what becomes Hydrophonie).
- *Zwingburgen des Hedonismus* (1987, Multimood).
- *Notturno* (1986/1987, Discos Esplendor Geometrico) — electronically manipulated piano: *Erste/Zweite/Dritte/Vierte/Fünfte Studie für Klavier*, *Studie für Henry*, *Drei Beisetzungen in Wien*, *Die Krämer im Tempel*. The eight "Studien für Klavier" are the clearest manifestation of his "album as collected études" form.
- *Aus Freude am Elend* (1988, DOM) — sample-based "plunderphonics" using pop-vocal fragments (Scaruffi).
- *E* (1988, DOM) — with Okko Bekker.
- *Marches Funèbres* (1989, Multimood) — funereal, percussion-and-electronics, called by Scaruffi his "most musical" composition.

(The title **"Geographik"** flagged in the brief: *uncertain — verify; I cannot locate a Tietchens album of that name in Discogs / AllMusic / Scaruffi / Bandcamp. May be a confusion with another DOM-era release, or with a track title. Should be considered unconfirmed.*)

#### Phase 3 — Die Stadt / Selektion / 1990s formalism
Long collaboration with Achim Wollscheid (Selektion, Frankfurt); reissue programme on Die Stadt; collaborations with PBK, Vidna Obmana, Arcane Device, RLW. Increasingly austere.

- *Stupor Mundi* (1990, Discos Esplendor Geometrico) — pounding, churning drone-noise; sometimes described as a follow-up to *Geboren um zu dienen* (1986, *uncertain — verify exact year*) on the same label.
- *Sinkende Schwimmer* (1991, Barooni) — thirteen 1960s-style tape miniatures.
- *Daseinsverfehlung* ("life-failure", 1992, label *uncertain — verify; Discogs lists a Daseinsverfehlung release but I did not pin down its label here*).
- *Seuchengebiete 2* (1992, Syrenia).
- *Five Manifestoes* (1992, Realization Records, with **PBK** — audio recorded 1990). *Note: brief assumed this was a Wollscheid / Selektion 1993 release; sources do not support that. Verified attribution is Tietchens + PBK, 1992.*
- *Tietchens / Vidna Obmana* (1992, Syrenia) — first of several Tietchens / Vidna Obmana releases.
- *Das Fest ist zu Ende* (1993, Barooni).
- *dbl.fdbk* (1993, Stille Andacht, with Arcane Device / David Lee Myers).
- *Speiseleitung* (1996, Raum 312 / Staalplaat, with Arcane Device).
- *Repetitive Movements* (1999, Staalplaat, with **Achim Wollscheid**).
- *Motives for Recycling* (1999, Soleilmoon, 2×CD with **Vidna Obmana**).

#### Phase 4 — Menge / Hydrophonie / Kontakt der Jünglinge / Line, 2000–
The mature reductive system. Two big serial cycles run in parallel:

The **Menge series** ("quantities"), built strictly from sine tones and white noise:
- *Alpha-Menge* (2000, Ritornell / Mille Plateaux) — contains an extended ~36-minute piece.
- *Beta-Menge* (2001, Ritornell).
- *Gamma-Menge* (2002, Ritornell).
- *Delta-Menge* (Ritornell, *uncertain — verify year, but bracketed by Beta and Epsilon*).
- *Epsilon-Menge* (Line, *uncertain — verify year*).
- *Zeta-Menge* (2006, Line) — tracks called "Teilmenge" ("partial sets"); the artist himself said he was working with "spatial structures without being 'spacy'... the room between foreground and background."
- *Eta-Menge* (2008, Line).
- *h-Menge*, *Z-menge* (Line, later in the series, dates *uncertain — verify*).

The **Hydrophonie series**, hydrophone (underwater microphone) recordings processed past recognition, numbered into the dozens (audible numbering reaches at least *Hydrophonie 26* / *Hydrophonie 32* on Auf Abwegen). Often gathered into the *Seuchengebiete* sequence:
- *Seuchengebiete* (1985, A-Mission) — Hydrophonie origin.
- *Seuchengebiete 2* (1992, Syrenia).
- *Seuchengebiete 3* (later, *uncertain — verify year*).
- *Seuchengebiete 4* (2021, Auf Abwegen) — collects Hydrophonie 21, 22, 24, 26, 27, 32.

The **Kontakt der Jünglinge** trio with **Thomas Köner**, named in deliberate ironic homage to Stockhausen's *Kontakte / Gesang der Jünglinge*:
- *Kontakt der Jünglinge* (2001, Die Stadt) — live.
- *-1* (2002, Die Stadt) — live.
- *N* (2003, Die Stadt) — live.

Other phase-4 work:
- *Adventures in Sound* (2003, Die Stadt) — collected very early tape experiments 1965–1969, a kind of pre-history archive.
- *60:00* (2004, Line, with Arcane Device).
- *Flussdichte* (2001, Disco Bruit, with David Lee Myers).
- *FT+* (2003, Crouton, collaboration).
- *Fabrication 2* (2011, Auf Abwegen, with **Richard Chartier**).
- *Flächen mit Figuren* (2009, label *uncertain — verify; Discogs hit but not fully read*).
- *Fundstück September 02* — Tietchens + Wollscheid, Klanggalerie, *uncertain — verify year*.

**Side-name projects** (kept separate from the "serious oeuvre"):
- **Liliental** — one self-titled LP (1978, Brain / Metronome) with Dieter Moebius, Conny Plank, Okko Bekker, Helmut Hattler, Johannes Pappert. A Krautrock supergroup one-off.
- **Hematic Sunsets** — Tietchens' own "song" / lounge-exotica project, used as a relief valve from his serious work.
- **Club of Rome** — *uncertain — verify; named as a Tietchens moniker in his Wikipedia page.*

### Aesthetic vocabulary

The terms in the task brief (Formhaft, Materialästhetik, glasklare Strukturen, Anti-Musik) circulate in critical writing about Tietchens (especially in *Aufabwegen* magazine and *Bad Alchemy*) but are not all directly attested as Tietchens' own words in the interviews I could access. What **is** verbatim from him:

- **"Absolute Music"** — his own preferred self-label. *"Absolute Music means that it contains no message except an esthetic one. Absolute Music is not connected with any political, spiritual, ideological or educational intention."* (Tietchens, Psychedelic Baby Magazine 2021)
- **Formalism over expression** — *"The 'formal exercises' mean that I try to achieve more or less variations based on one initial idea. I'm interested in creating structures and sounds which fit with my aforementioned esthetic. It is hard research work and it needs time, time and time again."* (OndaRock interview)
- **Anti-Programmusik** — *"I try to avoid 'picture music' ('Programmusik') as it was fashionable in the 19th century. Images, evocations and — yes — also moods have no business in my music."* (OndaRock)
- **"Multiplicity in the smallest perceptible sections of time and/or space"** — his own gloss on the Menge series: *"I continuously develop my idea of 'multiplicity in smallest perceptible sections of time and/or space'."* (OndaRock)
- **Ear, not algorithm** — *"I rely on my ears (!) and not (!) on algorithms or mathematics."* (OndaRock) — this is critical: Tietchens' aesthetic *looks* mathematical but is *audited* by ear, not generated by formula.
- **Slow work** — *"That needs time. I am and always was a very slow worker."* (OndaRock)
- **Spatial poetics without illusion** — on *Zeta-Menge*: he tried to create "spatial structures without being 'spacy'. There is an acoustic foreground and an acoustic background. My main interest is in the room between foreground and background." (paraphrased in multiple interviews; phrasing from Psychedelic Baby summary)
- **Scepticism as method** — *"Since 1985 I attach a Cioran quotation to my releases. ... Cioran was a — sceptic. I'm, too, a sceptic, and thus remain sceptical against Cioran."* (OndaRock / Psychedelic Baby)

**Critical-vocabulary terms (Formhaft, Materialästhetik, glasklare Strukturen, Anti-Musik)**: *uncertain — verify as Tietchens' own self-descriptions vs. critics' descriptions of him.* In *Aufabwegen* and German criticism these terms are widely used **about** Tietchens; they fit his stated practice but I could not in this pass quote them from his own mouth.

**Distinction from Schimpfluch-Gruppe (Rudolf Eb.er, Joke Lanz, Sudden Infant)**: Schimpfluch is transgressive, ritual-Aktionist, body-based, deliberately provocative; Tietchens is dry, structural, anti-expressive, never transgressive *for* transgression. The two scenes share the German experimental microcosm but represent opposite poles — Schimpfluch is *Aktion*, Tietchens is *Form*. (This is the consensus framing in *Bad Alchemy* and German criticism — *uncertain — verify with a specific citation*.)

### Technique (the load-bearing section for the composer agent)

1. **Source material — three families, kept clean**:
   - **Pure tones / sine clusters** — strict sine generators, often layered into beating clusters (the *Menge* world).
   - **Narrow-band noise / white noise** — used in the *Menge* series alongside sines, never as a wall, always as a thin band.
   - **Hydrophone recordings** — underwater microphone captures (the *Hydrophonie* / *Seuchengebiete* world), then processed by EQ, filtering, time-stretching, and gating until the water-origin is unrecognisable. This is concrète heritage (Schaeffer's *son fixé*) but stripped of any field-recording naïveté: the source is annihilated as recognisable image.

2. **Lineage of process — Schaefferian, but dry**:
   - From Schaeffer/GRM: the *objet sonore* — a sound is whatever it is, regardless of its cause; reduce listening to the sound itself.
   - From Köln (Eimert / Stockhausen): synthetic sources from first principles; constructed not captured.
   - Tietchens fuses both: concrète gestures with elektronische rigour. He is explicit about this debt: *"I was much more (in a way) fascinated by the so called Serious Music á la Pierre Schaeffer, the electronic music of the Cologne School and ideas of Joseph Beuys."* (OndaRock)

3. **Processing posture** — minimal but precise:
   - Heavy EQ shaping rather than spectacular FX.
   - Granulation present but always **controlled** — small grain populations, audible individual grains, never the dense "cloud" of GRM-school granulation.
   - Time-stretching to expose interior detail.
   - **Spatial dryness** — minimal reverb. When space is present it is the implied room of the studio itself, never a cathedral, never an imagined landscape. *"Spatial structures without being 'spacy'."*
   - **No tape hiss aesthetic** post-1990 — the surface is clean even when the contents are abrasive. This is the visible break from Schaeffer / Henry / early industrial.

4. **Frequency / amplitude design**:
   - Mid-low body, with occasional **ultrasonic-edge** shimmer (the very-high-end "edge" sine clusters in the *Menge* series).
   - Wide dynamic range available but used sparingly. Long quiet stretches → short sharp interruptions.
   - Sustained tones often hold for tens of seconds, broken by brief percussive transients rather than gestural development.

5. **Silence as structural element**:
   - Silence punctuates, separates, *frames* — never as the absent-presence rhetoric of Wandelweiser (Frey, Beuger, Pisaro), where silence is a substance in itself. Tietchens uses silence the way concrete poets use page-space: a structural pause. Adjacent to Wandelweiser, not Wandelweiser.

6. **Form-level technique**:
   - Composition by *variation on a single initial idea* (his own term, OndaRock).
   - Album = collection of études on one constraint (cf. the eight *Studien für Klavier* on *Notturno*; the seven *Teilmenge* on *Beta-Menge*).
   - **Numbered / abstract titling** (*Teilmenge 1–7*, *Studie für Klavier 1–5*, *Hydrophonie 1–32+*) — the title carries the catalogue, not the meaning.

### Form

- **Typical durations**: individual tracks 2–9 minutes; long pieces 20–40 minutes (e.g. *Alpha-Menge*'s ~36' piece); CD wholes ~50–60 minutes; series-structures cumulatively gigantic (the Hydrophonie series alone exceeds 30 entries).
- **Sectional behaviour**: blocks rather than developments. A texture is established, holds, is interrupted, is replaced by another block. No goal-directed motion, no climax, no recapitulation.
- **"Album as collected études" pattern**: the typical Tietchens record is *N* short-to-medium pieces, each a single operation applied to a single material, gathered under one title that names the operation rather than the affect (*Hydrophonie*, *Menge*, *Studien für Klavier*).
- **No narrative, no programme, no story** — explicit and repeated.

### Philosophical / theoretical frame

1. **Adorno's *Materialdenken*** — the materialist position that musical material has its own historical truth and cannot be wished into expressing what the composer wants. Tietchens fits this frame: the sound-material is treated as a fact to be worked, not a vehicle to be loaded with intention. *Whether Tietchens cites Adorno explicitly: uncertain — verify.*
2. **Anti-romantic, anti-expressive** — directly stated and self-consciously maintained.
3. **Concrete Poetry (Hans G Helms, Helmut Heißenbüttel)** — the conceptual parallel is strong (material treated as object, abstract titling, serial systems applied to small units, refusal of representation). *Direct attestation in Tietchens' interviews: uncertain — verify.*
4. **Lachenmann's *musique concrète instrumentale*** — both projects share the conviction that sound material is the compositional substance, but Lachenmann derives sound from instrumental technique and Tietchens from electronic / hydrophonic sources. Parallel, not lineage.
5. **Serial-thought background** — the *appearance* of serial / set-theoretic logic (numbered Teilmenge, systematic permutation) but Tietchens *rejects systematic generation* and audits everything by ear (*"I rely on my ears (!) and not (!) on algorithms or mathematics"*).
6. **E. M. Cioran** — Tietchens has attached Cioran quotations to his releases **since 1985**; the philosophical mood is scepticism, exhaustion, the refusal of redemption. This is the affective register *behind* the formal surface, even though the music itself does not "express" Cioran — it refuses expression while sharing his temperature.

- Pierre Henry — concrète manipulation as compositional substance.
- Karlheinz Stockhausen — *Studie I / II* (1953–54), *Kontakte* (1958–60); the Köln school of constructed electronic sound (Tietchens names the ironic *Kontakt der Jünglinge* trio after Stockhausen).
- Herbert Eimert — Köln Studio founding figure; Tietchens explicitly cites him: *"Before Stockhausen recorded his 'Studie 1 + 2'... composers like Herbert Eimert... already have created electronic music based on the theories of Werner Meyer-Eppler."* (Psychedelic Baby)

### Why it matters — handoff paragraph for the composer agent

The **transferable principles** that can be lifted out of Tietchens' practice and extended into a complete methodology (i.e. the seeds the next agent should compress into a system) are:

1. **Material discipline.** A small, well-defined sound-material set, deliberately impoverished, is treated as fact rather than vehicle. (For SuperCollider: a tight palette of generators — sines, narrow-band noise, hydrophone-like sample sources — and a refusal to grow that palette during a piece.)
2. **Ear-audited formalism.** Variation-on-an-initial-idea, but the variations are *selected* by ear, not generated by algorithm. (For SC: parametric generators are fine; *deterministic* generators are not the aesthetic; the system should expose decisions, not hide them in stochastic clouds.)
3. **Numbered études as form.** "Album as set of études" — *N* short pieces, each one operation on one material, gathered under one constraint-name. Maps cleanly onto a SuperCollider piece structured as a Pdef/Pdef-array of variations, each a *Teilmenge*-style operation.
4. **Spatial dryness.** Almost no reverb; the studio's implied room only; the "room between foreground and background" is the compositional space, not a simulated landscape. (For SC: gain-staging budget excludes large reverb; any spatialisation is dry pan, brief delay, narrow-band shimmer at most.)
5. **Silence as structural pause.** Block-form, not goal-form: textures begin, hold, are interrupted, are replaced. No development, no climax. (For SC: schedule by block rather than by envelope-curve.)
6. **Abstract numerical titling.** *Teilmenge n*, *Hydrophonie n*, *Studie n* — the title is the catalogue position, not a poetic image. (For SC: name SynthDefs and Pdefs after operations / sets, not after evocations.)
7. **Concrète heritage stripped of nostalgia.** Source recordings (water, objects, room) treated until origin is no longer audible; no field-recording sentimentality, no "place" rhetoric. (For SC: any sample-based source is filtered, granulated, EQ'd, time-stretched until it is *material*, not *image*.)
8. **Scepticism as compositional temperature.** Cioran-side affect: no redemption arc, no consolation, no narrative resolution. The music ends because it ends.
9. **A clear rejection of two adjacent traps**: Wandelweiser's silence-as-substance (Tietchens uses silence structurally, not metaphysically) and Schimpfluch's transgression (Tietchens is dry, not aktionistic). The system should refuse both temptations.
10. **Numbered serial expansion as career-architecture.** *Hydrophonie* and *Menge* are open-ended sets that can grow indefinitely. (For SC: design a piece-system as an open numbered series, not as a single closed work — instances are *episodes of one ongoing constraint*.)

The composer agent should treat these ten principles as the *core* — and then extend beyond Tietchens into the territory he refused to occupy (e.g. longer durations, denser polyphony, just-intonation cluster work, spatialisation that Tietchens explicitly avoided). The goal downstream is not to imitate Tietchens — it is to inherit his discipline and apply it to questions he did not address.
